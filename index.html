The Great UGA Transition

Going off to college was something that I have looked forward to since I was a little kid. The whole idea of moving somewhere new and being on my own sounded exciting, something that most people are terrified of.

When the day finally came, my excitement was mixed with nervousness and sadness about living 3 hours away from everyone I love. Move in day began with 3 vehicles packed full of all my things and 10 of my family members. As we began unloading and setting everything up I was very overwheled and really just wanted them all to leave already. I realized that they were just trying to help and that they were going to miss me so I just let them all do whatever they wanted to help. When it was time to say goobye everyone hugged me and started crying. At this point I realized that this whole living far away thing was going to be harder than I thought.

(Actual picture from the hectic move in day)

It quickly became easier, as I became more comfortable and made new friends. I have became so comfortable that I don't miss living at home. Sure I miss my friends and family, but I'm glad I escaped my small hometown to go to college.
